# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-01 — MORNING BRIEF REVIEW</span>

---

**Purpose:** Start every shift with a complete property overview in under 2 minutes.

**When:** Daily, at the start of the GM's shift or first thing each morning.

**Steps:**

1. Log in at `attendaapp.com/staff` with your Admin credentials.
2. The **Dashboard** tab loads automatically.
3. Review **GM Notes** at the top — check if the previous manager left any carryover items.
4. Read the **Quick Stats** tiles: Occupancy / Arrivals / Departures / Pending Requests.
5. Scroll to **Pending Requests** — address any items open for more than 30 minutes.
6. Check **Shuttle Times** — confirm today's scheduled runs match expected guest demand.
7. Review the **Forecast Summary** — compare forecast arrivals to current occupancy.
8. Post your own **GM Note** if there are important items for the team (see SOP-GM-02).

> **Tip:** Use the ⚙ Widget Customizer (gear icon, top right) to show only the sections that matter to your morning routine.

---

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-02 — POSTING A GM NOTE</span>

---

**Purpose:** Communicate critical property information to all staff on all shifts.

**Steps:**

1. Navigate to the **Dashboard** tab.
2. Locate the **GM Notes** section.
3. Click the **Edit / + New Note** button.
4. Type your message in the text field.
5. Click **Post** — the note is immediately visible to all logged-in staff.

> **Examples of GM Notes:**
> - "Pool maintenance until 2pm. Advise guests."
> - "VIP group (Rooms 201–210) checking in at 3pm. White-glove service."
> - "Fire alarm test scheduled 10am. Notify all guests."

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-03 — REVENUE DASHBOARD</span>

---

**Purpose:** Monitor all revenue flowing through Attenda — food orders, shuttle bookings, and transport requests — and verify the 10% commission model is working correctly.

**Access:** Admin → Revenue (in the left navigation under Admin section)

**Steps:**

1. Click **Revenue** in the Admin section of the left navigation.
2. Select a time period using the filter bar:
   - **This Week** — Monday to today
   - **This Month** — 1st to today
   - **All Time** — no date filter
3. Review the **three KPI cards** at the top:
   - **Total Revenue** — gross amount from all orders
   - **Hotel Commission** — your 10% cut
   - **Transactions** — total order count
4. Scroll down to the **Partner Breakdown Table** — review each partner's orders, gross revenue, and commission.
5. Note the **Shuttle Revenue** row beneath the partner table.
6. Review the **Daily Bar Chart** at the bottom — identify high-revenue days and patterns.

> **Action trigger:** If a partner's commission shows $0, verify they have active menu items and are receiving orders in their Vendor Portal.

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-04 — MANAGING STAFF CALLOUTS</span>

---

**Purpose:** Acknowledge and respond to staff callouts to ensure shift coverage.

**Access:** Admin → Callouts (notification badge shows pending count)

**Steps:**

**To review pending callouts:**
1. Click **Callouts** in the Admin section — the badge shows how many are unresolved.
2. Each callout card shows: staff name, date, reason, affected shift.
3. Choose an action:

| Action | When to Use | What It Does |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Approve** | Callout acknowledged, shift will not be covered | Closes the callout, removes from pending |
| **Find Cover** | Need to fill the shift | Opens Schedules filtered to that date |

**To find cover for a shift:**
1. Click **Find Cover** on the callout.
2. Schedules view opens for the callout date.
3. Identify available staff — check who is not already scheduled.
4. Click **+ Add Shift** and schedule the covering staff member.
5. Return to Callouts and click **Approve** to close the callout.

> **Note:** A red dot on the Schedules tab date header indicates a callout exists for that day.

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-05 — STAFF SCHEDULE MANAGEMENT</span>

---

**Purpose:** Build, edit, and publish weekly staff schedules.

**Access:** Operations → Schedules

### A. Viewing the Schedule

1. Click **Schedules** in the Operations section.
2. The weekly grid shows Mon–Sun across the top and staff members down the left.
3. Use the **← →** week arrows to navigate to any week.

### B. Adding a Shift

1. Click **+ Add Shift** in the header.
2. Select the **Staff Member** from the dropdown — Role auto-fills from their profile.
3. Set **Date**, **Start Time**, and **End Time**.
4. For housekeeping staff: check **"Open end time"** if the shift end is unknown — shows as `→ TBD` in amber.
5. Add optional **Notes** (e.g., "Cover front desk during lunch").
6. Click **Save Shift**.

### C. Editing a Shift

1. Click on any existing shift card on the grid.
2. The **Edit Shift modal** opens with all fields pre-filled.
3. Modify any field (time, role, notes).
4. Click **Save Changes** to update, or **Delete Shift** (red button) to remove.

> ⚠️ **Delete is permanent.** The Save and Delete buttons are intentionally separated to prevent accidents.

### D. Copy to Next Week

1. Navigate to the week you want to copy from.
2. Click **Copy to Next Week** in the header.
3. If next week already has shifts, confirm the prompt.
4. All shifts are duplicated 7 days forward.

### E. Schedule Templates

**Save a template:**
1. Build or navigate to a schedule week.
2. Click **Save as Template** (bookmark icon).
3. Enter a name (e.g., "Standard Summer Week").
4. Click Save.

**Load a template:**
1. Navigate to the target week.
2. Click **Load Template** (folder icon).
3. Select a template from the list → click **Apply**.
4. Confirm if the week already has shifts.

### F. Publishing the Schedule

1. Review the week's shifts.
2. Click **Publish** — staff can now see their shifts.
3. Staff receive no automatic notification — post a GM Note if needed.

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-06 — PARTNER & VENDOR MANAGEMENT</span>

---

**Purpose:** Manage the hotel's restaurant and service partner network.

**Access:** Admin → Partners

### A. Reviewing Applications

1. Click **Partners** in the Admin section.
2. Click the **Applications** sub-tab.
3. Review each pending application: business name, type, contact, description.
4. Click **Approve** to create the vendor account (applicant is notified via email) or **Reject** to archive it.

### B. Sharing the Apply Link

1. In the Partners tab, click **Share Apply Link**.
2. The URL `https://attendaapp.com/apply?hotel={your-slug}` is copied to clipboard.
3. Share via email, WhatsApp, or printed materials with prospective partners.

### C. Managing Active Partners

1. In the Partners tab, click a partner name.
2. Edit their name, hours, description, or category.
3. Toggle **Active/Inactive** to control their visibility to guests.
4. Click the **Menu** sub-tab to add, edit, or remove menu items.

### D. Running a Test Order

1. Open a partner's detail page.
2. Click **Test Order** — places a $0 test order through the full guest ordering flow.
3. Verify the order appears in the Vendor Portal and staff requests feed.

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-07 — STAFF ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT</span>

---

**Purpose:** Create and manage staff accounts for the property.

**Access:** Admin → Staff

### Adding a Staff Member

1. Click **Staff** in the Admin section.
2. Click **Add Staff Member**.
3. Fill in:

| Field | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Name | Full name |
| Email | Used for login |
| Password | Temporary — staff should change on first login |
| Role | admin / manager / staff / vendor |
| Department | front_desk / housekeeping / maintenance / food_beverage / drivers / management / other |

4. Click **Create** — the account is immediately active.
5. Share login credentials with the staff member securely.

### Removing a Staff Member

1. Find the staff member in the list.
2. Click the **Delete** (trash) icon.
3. Confirm deletion — the account is deactivated immediately.

> **Note:** Deleting a staff account does not delete historical records associated with that account.

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-09 — KPI REVIEW & MANAGEMENT</span>

---

**Purpose:** Monitor daily performance metrics across all departments.

**Access:** Operations → KPIs

### Reviewing Today's KPIs

1. Click **KPIs** in the Operations section.
2. Select a category tab (Revenue, Operations, Guest Experience, etc.).
3. Review submitted values — green checkmarks indicate completion, amber indicates pending.
4. Click any metric to see the submission history and trend.

### Managing KPI Definitions

1. In the KPIs tab, click **Manage KPIs** (Admin only).
2. To add a new KPI: click **New KPI**, enter name, category, target value, and frequency.
3. To deactivate a KPI: toggle the Active switch off.

### Installing a KPI Pack from Marketplace

1. Navigate to **Marketplace** in the left navigation.
2. Browse available KPI packs.
3. Click a pack → **Install** — KPIs are added to your active configuration immediately.

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-10 — FORECAST SUBMISSION</span>

---

**Purpose:** Input daily occupancy forecast to drive staffing recommendations.

**Access:** Operations → Forecast

### Submitting a Forecast

1. Click **Forecast** in the Operations section.
2. Click **New Forecast** or click a date on the monthly calendar.
3. Fill in:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Date | The forecast date |
| Expected Occupancy | Rooms occupied (integer) |
| Arrivals | Expected check-ins |
| Departures | Expected check-outs |
| Group Blocks | Rooms blocked for groups |
| VIP Arrivals | VIP/loyalty guest count |
| Notes | Events, demand drivers |

4. Click **Submit Forecast**.
5. Optional: click **Generate Shifts** to auto-suggest staffing based on occupancy ratios.

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-11 — COMPSET RATE MONITORING</span>

---

**Purpose:** Track competitor hotel rates to inform your own pricing strategy.

**Access:** Operations → Compset

### Logging a Compset Call

1. Click **Compset** in the Operations section.
2. Click a competitor property.
3. Click **Log Call**.
4. Enter today's quoted rates for each room category.
5. Add notes (promotions, restrictions, availability).
6. Click **Save**.

### Reviewing the Rate Position

1. In Compset, view the **Rate Comparison** table showing your rates vs. the market.
2. A green indicator means you are priced below the market average.
3. A red indicator means you are priced above.

# <span style="color: #7B1C3E;">SOP-GM-12 — CULTURE HUB MANAGEMENT</span>

---

**Purpose:** Manage team engagement, rewards, and recognition.

**Access:** Operations → Culture Hub

### Awarding Points to Staff

1. Click **Culture Hub** in the Operations section.
2. Click the **Leaderboard** tab.
3. Click a staff member's name → **Award Points**.
4. Enter point amount and reason.
5. Click **Award** — points appear on the leaderboard immediately.

### Managing the Rewards Catalog

1. In Culture Hub, click the **Rewards** tab.
2. Click **Add Reward**.
3. Enter name, point cost, description, and quantity.
4. Click Save.

### Approving Reward Redemptions

1. In Rewards tab, click **Pending Redemptions**.
2. Review each request — staff member, reward, points to deduct.
3. Click **Approve** or **Reject** (with optional note).

### Posting a Team Event

1. In Culture Hub, click the **Events** tab.
2. Click **New Event**.
3. Enter title, date/time, location, and description.
4. Click **Post** — visible to all staff immediately.

---
